Details of The Simpsons

The most famous family in Springfield and the world. The animated comedy that continues to make history. Homer works at the nuclear plant and is not your typical family man: although he has the best intentions, they are not always enough. Marge is the loving, blue-haired matriarch; Bart, the troubled son; Lisa, the outstanding daughter; and Maggie, the baby who never talks. In short: The Simpsons!

The multi-award-winning series over the years at the Annie, Peabody and Emmy Awards, among others, won the Emmy for Best Animated Series again in 2019. Since it surpassed “The Law of the Revolver” in number of primetime episodes, it is the longest-running scripted series in small screen history. Produced by Gracie Films in association with 20th Century Fox Television.

Most Relevant Episodes

Homer's Phobia

This episode is significant for its sensitive and positive portrayal of homosexuality and confronting homophobia. It features John Waters as a guest star and explores themes of tolerance and acceptance.

S8, E15

Marge vs. the Monorail

Widely considered one of the show's funniest and most iconic episodes. It's a brilliant satire of civic boosterism and features a memorable guest appearance by Leonard Nimoy. The catchy song and absurd plotline are hallmarks of the show's golden era.

S4, E12

Who Shot Mr. Burns? (Part One)

This episode is a significant moment in Simpsons history due to its cliffhanger ending and the subsequent "whodunit" mystery that unfolded over the summer before the next season's premiere. It's a well-crafted episode that builds suspense and allows for speculation about the culprit.

S6, E25

Last Exit to Springfield

Often cited by critics and fans as the best Simpsons episode of all time. It's a clever and hilarious story about the union workers at the Springfield Nuclear Power Plant going on strike, with Mr. Burns as the antagonist. It's full of memorable lines and situations.

S4, E17

22 Short Films About Springfield

This unique episode breaks away from the traditional narrative structure to present a series of short vignettes focusing on various residents of Springfield. It's a creative way to showcase the show's large ensemble cast and its diverse humor. The "Steamed Hams" segment has become a particularly enduring and meme-worthy part of internet culture.

S7, E21

Lisa's Substitute

A more emotionally resonant episode that deals with themes of mentorship, loss, and the impact of a good teacher. It features Dustin Hoffman (as Sam Etic, a pseudonym) as Lisa's substitute teacher, Mr. Bergstrom, with whom she forms a deep connection.

Simpsons Characters

Homer Simpson

The bumbling patriarch of the family, known for his love of donuts and beer.

Marge Simpson

The loving and patient mother with her iconic blue beehive hairstyle.

Bart Simpson

The mischievous and rebellious son, famous for his catchphrase "Eat my shorts!"

Lisa Simpson

The intelligent and socially conscious daughter, often seen playing her saxophone.

Maggie Simpson

The silent but adorable baby of the family, known for her pacifier.

Montgomery Burns

The silent but adorable baby of the family, known for her pacifier.The billionaire owner of the Springfield Nuclear Power Plant and Homer Simpson's boss.

Moe Szyslak

The grumpy and cynical owner and bartender of Moe's Tavern, Homer and his friends' favorite bar.

Barney Gumble

Homer Simpson's best friend and Springfield's resident drunk, who spends most of his time at Moe's Tavern.

Apu Nahasapeemapetilon

The hardworking and often overwhelmed owner of the Kwik-E-Mart convenience store.

Ned Flanders

The overly optimistic, pious, and well-meaning next-door neighbor of the Simpsons, whose morality and kindness often exasperate Homer.

Chief Wiggum

The clumsy, gluttonous, and incompetent police chief of Springfield.

Milhouse Van Houten

Bart Simpson's best friend, he is a loyal, often unlucky, and low self-esteem child.

Nelson Muntz

The bully of Springfield Elementary School, famous for his signature laugh: "Ha-ha!"

Principal Skinner (Seymour Skinner)

The strict and often frustrated principal of Springfield Elementary School, constantly dealing with Bart and the students' antics.

Waylon Smithers

Mr. Burns' loyal and subservient personal assistant and devoted admirer.

Krusty the Clown

The host of a popular children's television show, he is a cynical, heavy-smoking clown with a chaotic personal life.
